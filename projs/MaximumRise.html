Problem 3.1 Stock Analysis by Maximum Rise

We are writing a small piece of code to help analyze the rise of a stock price over a period of time to support investment decisions. We are given time series data for the prices of a particular stock, represented as a list where each value is the stock price at a specific time point. The time points are represented by indices: 0, 1, 2, ... . As a heuristic to evaluate the potential of the stock, we aim to compute the maximum rise in its price between any two time points.

Specifically, given a list \( p \) of \( N \) time points, we want to find indices \( i \) and \( j \) (where \( 0 \leq i < j < N \)) such that \( p[j] - p[i] \) is the maximum among all possible \( i, j \) pairs.
