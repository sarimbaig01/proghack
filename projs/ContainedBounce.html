Contained Bounce

Introduction

In this exercise, you will implement a program where a ball bounces freely within a graphics window. The ball is modeled as a circle and, at all times, it maintains a constant speed and one of eight possible directions: up, down, left, right, down-right, down-left, up-right, and up-left. The ball will start at a random point on the screen, moving in one of these eight directions.

The graphics window for this exercise has dimensions of 800 pixels in width and 600 pixels in height. The coordinate system of the window places the origin (0, 0) at the top-left corner, with the other corners defined as follows: the top-right corner is at (799, 0), the bottom-left corner is at (0, 599), and the bottom-right corner is at (799, 599).

As the ball moves within the window, the application will need to detect when the ball collides with any of the window's sides. Upon collision, the ball should rebound following the basic laws of reflection of light from a smooth surface. We simplify the physics of the bouncing ball by mapping it to the reflection of light. The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection, and the incident ray, reflected ray, and the normal to the surface at the point of incidence all lie in the same plane.
